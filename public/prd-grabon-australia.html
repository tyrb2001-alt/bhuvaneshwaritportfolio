GrabOn Australia — Launch PRD

Launch GrabOn in Australia as a coupon-first platform, starting with the Indian diaspora, then expanding to mainstream shoppers.

Owner
Product Manager
Status
Proposed
Timeline
12 months
Market
Australia

Summary

Don't fight ShopBack and Cashrewards on cashback. Launch GrabOn as a coupon-first site for Australia's ~800K–1M Indian community, using GrabOn's existing AI content stack to keep costs low. Prove it, then expand.

Problem

  • ShopBack and Cashrewards dominate cashback but have a trust gap: 30–90 day payout waits and disputes.
  • OzBargain wins on community but has no curation, no personalisation, no dedicated team.
  • No one is serving the Indian diaspora shopper deliberately, a large, digitally-active, value-conscious group.

Goals & Non-Goals

Goals

  • Win the Indian diaspora beachhead in Year 1.
  • Prove unit economics (CAC < commission per user).
  • Build an SEO content moat cheaply using AI agents.
  • Sign 15–20 anchor merchants before public launch.

Non-Goals (V1)

  • No cashback product.
  • No broad paid-media brand war.
  • No GrabCash or GrabShare in Australia yet.
  • No mainstream categories in first 6 months.

Target User

Primary: Indian-origin shopper in Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth (age 22–45).

They shop for:

  • Flights to India, remittance-related services
  • Ethnic grocery and specialty retail
  • International telecom and prepaid plans
  • Electronics, gadgets, festival gifting (Diwali, Holi)

Secondary (Phase 2): international students, wider South/East Asian diaspora.

User Stories

As a diaspora shopper, I want a verified coupon that works at checkout so I save money instantly, no 90-day wait.
As a Diwali shopper, I want curated festival deals across flights, gifts and grocery in one place.
As a student, I want student-relevant deals on telecom, electronics and travel.
As a merchant, I want a low-effort partner that brings a high-intent audience I don't reach today.

Rollout Plan

Phase 0 · Months −2 to 0

Quiet build

Localise stack (AUD, GST). Sign 15–20 anchor merchants. Pre-build SEO library.

Phase 1 · Months 0–3

Beachhead launch

Launch via diaspora WhatsApp/Facebook groups and micro-influencers. Time public push around Diwali.

Phase 2 · Months 3–6

Adjacent segments

Extend to international students and wider South/East Asian diaspora.

Phase 3 · Months 6–12

Go mainstream

Only after CAC and retention are proven, broaden to fashion, home, everyday e-com.

Assumptions & Risks

Assumptions

  • Blended merchant commission ~5–7% of GMV.
  • 800K–1M Indian-origin AU population, digitally active.
  • AI agent stack ports to AU at low marginal cost.
  • Lean team of 4–5 people is enough for Year 1.

Risks

  • Diaspora CAC higher than modelled, pause Phase 2.
  • Merchant sign-ups slower than expected.
  • Incumbents copy the diaspora angle.
  • SEO compounding takes longer than 6 months.

Kill / Rethink Signal

If cost per transacting user > 2× commission per user by day 90, or Day-60 repeat rate < 15%, pause Phase 2 spend and revisit segment or model before scaling further.
